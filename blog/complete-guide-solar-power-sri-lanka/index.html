Solar Power Basics: How It Works in Simple Terms

You do not need an engineering degree to understand solar power. Here is how a typical Sri Lankan solar system works.

The Basic Process:

Step 1: Sunlight Hits Solar Panels

Photovoltaic (PV) cells in the panels convert sunlight into direct current (DC) electricity. Even on cloudy days, panels produce power (though at reduced capacity). Colombo's tropical climate provides consistent year-round production.

Step 2: Inverter Converts DC to AC

Your home and appliances use alternating current (AC) electricity. The inverter converts DC from panels into usable AC power. Modern inverters also monitor system performance and safety.

Step 3: Power Flows to Your Home

Solar electricity powers your lights, appliances, and devices first. Any excess power is either stored in batteries or exported to the grid. When solar production is low (night, heavy rain), you draw from the grid or batteries.

Step 4: Net Metering Tracks Excess Production

A bi-directional meter measures both power you use and power you export. CEB credits you for exported energy, reducing your monthly bill. Credits roll over month to month, maximizing your savings.

Sun → Solar Panels → Inverter → Home → Grid/Battery

Key Terms Explained:

  • kW (Kilowatt): Measure of power output; typical home systems range from 3kW to 10kW
  • kWh (Kilowatt-hour): Measure of energy produced or consumed; your electricity bill is based on kWh
  • Peak Sun Hours: Hours per day when sunlight is strong enough for maximum panel production (5-6 in Colombo)
  • Self-Consumption: Percentage of solar power you use directly vs. export to grid

Three Solar System Types: Which Is Right for Your Property?

Sri Lankan properties typically use one of three solar configurations. Each has different costs, benefits, and suitability depending on your goals and grid reliability.

System Type 1: Grid-Tied Solar (Most Popular for Cost Savings)

Solar panels connected directly to the CEB grid with net metering capability. No battery storage.

How It Works:

  • Solar powers your home during daylight hours
  • Excess energy exports to CEB grid for credit
  • At night or during low production, you draw from the grid using accumulated credits
  • System automatically shuts off during grid outages for safety (anti-islanding)

Best For:

  • Properties with reliable grid connection
  • Homeowners primarily seeking bill reduction
  • Daytime electricity users (home offices, air conditioning)
  • Budget-conscious installations (lowest upfront cost)

Typical Investment (2026):

  • 3kW System: LKR 350,000-550,000 all-inclusive
  • 5kW System: LKR 550,000-850,000 all-inclusive
  • 10kW System: LKR 1,000,000-1,600,000 all-inclusive

Key Benefit: Lowest upfront cost with immediate bill reduction through net metering credits.

System Type 2: Hybrid Solar with Battery Backup (Most Versatile)

Grid-tied solar system integrated with battery storage for power continuity during outages.

How It Works:

  • Solar charges batteries during daylight
  • Batteries supply critical loads during grid outages or load-shedding
  • Grid supplements when solar and batteries are insufficient
  • Smart inverter manages power flow between solar, battery, and grid

Best For:

  • Properties experiencing frequent power interruptions
  • Critical load protection needs (home office, medical equipment, security)
  • Homeowners wanting maximum energy independence
  • Properties with daytime AND nighttime usage patterns

Typical Investment (2026):

  • 3kW System + 5kWh Battery: LKR 550,000-900,000 all-inclusive
  • 5kW System + 10kWh Battery: LKR 900,000-1,400,000 all-inclusive
  • Additional battery capacity: LKR 80,000-150,000 per kWh

Key Benefit: Bill reduction plus reliable backup power during load-shedding and outages.

System Type 3: Off-Grid Solar (Specialized Applications)

Standalone solar system with battery storage, completely independent from CEB grid.

How It Works:

  • Solar charges batteries exclusively
  • Batteries supply all property loads
  • Generator backup optional for extended cloudy periods
  • No grid connection or net metering involvement

Best For:

  • Remote properties without grid access
  • Agricultural or industrial sites with dedicated power needs
  • Maximum energy independence requirements
  • Properties where grid connection is unreliable or prohibitively expensive

Typical Investment: Quoted per property after detailed load analysis and site assessment

Key Benefit: Complete energy independence with no utility bills.

CEB Net Metering in Sri Lanka: Step-by-Step Application Guide

Net metering allows you to export excess solar energy to the CEB grid and receive credit on your electricity bill. Here is exactly how the application process works in 2026.

What Is Net Metering?

Net metering is a billing arrangement with Ceylon Electricity Board (CEB) that:

  • Credits you for excess solar energy exported to the grid
  • Allows credits to roll over month to month
  • Reduces or eliminates your electricity bill depending on production vs. usage
  • Requires a bi-directional meter installed by CEB

Eligibility Requirements:

  • Property must have a valid CEB electricity account in your name
  • Electrical installation must comply with Sri Lankan wiring regulations
  • System size must not exceed your sanctioned load capacity (typically 1:1 ratio)
  • Property must have adequate roof or ground space for panel installation
  • Installation must be performed by a licensed electrical contractor

Step-by-Step Application Process:

Step 1: Pre-Application Assessment (1-2 days)

  • Six7 Electrical conducts site assessment to verify system feasibility
  • We review your electricity bills to determine appropriate system size
  • We confirm your sanctioned load capacity with CEB records
  • We provide preliminary system design and cost estimate

Step 2: Documentation Preparation (2-3 days)

We prepare all required documents:

  • Completed CEB net metering application form
  • Single-line electrical diagram of proposed system
  • Equipment specifications and certifications
  • Property ownership documentation or landlord consent
  • Licensed contractor credentials (Six7 Electrical)

We review documents with you for accuracy and signatures.

Step 3: Application Submission (Day 1 of processing)

  • We submit your complete application package to CEB through appropriate channels
  • We provide you with application reference number for tracking
  • CEB acknowledges receipt and begins technical review

Step 4: CEB Technical Review (2-6 weeks typical)

  • CEB engineers review system design for grid compatibility
  • They may request additional information or minor design adjustments
  • We coordinate any required revisions on your behalf
  • You receive status updates throughout the review process

Step 5: Approval and Meter Installation (1-2 weeks after approval)

  • Upon approval, CEB schedules installation of bi-directional meter
  • A CEB technician visits your property to install and commission the meter
  • We coordinate final system commissioning with meter activation
  • Your net metering account becomes active

Step 6: System Activation and Monitoring

  • Your solar system begins exporting excess power to the grid
  • Your first net metering bill reflects credits for exported energy
  • We provide monitoring setup and user training
  • Ongoing support for any billing or technical questions

Typical Timeline Summary:

  • Preparation and submission: 1 week
  • CEB review and approval: 2-6 weeks (varies by region and application volume)
  • Meter installation and activation: 1-2 weeks
  • Total typical timeline: 4-9 weeks from application to active net metering

Important Notes:

  • System installation can proceed while net metering application processes
  • You can use solar power immediately; credits begin once net metering is active
  • Application fees (if any) are typically minimal and included in our service
  • CEB policies may change; we stay current and guide you through updates

Common Application Challenges and Solutions:

  • Challenge: Sanctioned load capacity too low for desired system size

  • Challenge: Property documentation not in applicant's name
    Solution: Provide landlord consent letter or update CEB account ownership
  • Challenge: Technical review requests design modifications
    Solution: Our engineering team revises design promptly to meet CEB requirements

How to Size Your Solar System: Match Production to Your Usage

Choosing the right system size maximizes your savings while avoiding unnecessary upfront costs. Here is how to determine the optimal size for your Colombo property.

Step 1: Analyze Your Electricity Usage

Gather Your Data:

  • Collect 12 months of electricity bills to understand seasonal variations
  • Note your average monthly kWh consumption (found on each bill)
  • Identify your peak usage periods (daytime vs. nighttime)
  • Calculate your average daily consumption: Monthly kWh / 30 days

Example Calculation:

  • Average monthly usage: 450 kWh
  • Average daily usage: 450 / 30 = 15 kWh per day
  • Peak sun hours in Colombo: 5-6 hours
  • Minimum system size needed: 15 kWh / 5 hours = 3 kW

Step 2: Consider Your Usage Patterns

Daytime-Heavy Usage (Ideal for Solar):

  • Home office, air conditioning, water heating during daylight
  • Higher self-consumption ratio means more immediate savings
  • Smaller system may suffice since you use power as it is produced

Nighttime-Heavy Usage (Consider Hybrid or Larger System):

  • Evening cooking, lighting, entertainment after sunset
  • Lower self-consumption means more reliance on net metering credits
  • Larger system or battery storage may improve economics

Balanced Usage (Most Common):

  • Mix of daytime and nighttime consumption
  • Grid-tied system with net metering typically optimal
  • Consider battery if backup power is valuable to you

Step 3: Account for Future Changes

Factors That May Increase Future Usage:

  • Adding air conditioning or electric water heating
  • Purchasing an electric vehicle (adds 3-7 kWh/day charging needs)
  • Home expansion or additional occupants
  • Working from home more frequently

Recommendation: Size your system with 10-20% growth capacity if you anticipate increased usage.

Step 4: Evaluate Physical Constraints

Roof Space Requirements:

  • Typical panel: ~1.7 square meters, ~400W output
  • 3kW system: ~8-10 panels, ~14-17 square meters
  • 5kW system: ~13-15 panels, ~22-26 square meters
  • Allow 10-15% extra space for maintenance access

Roof Orientation and Shading:

  • South-facing roofs produce maximum annual output in Sri Lanka
  • East or west-facing still viable with slightly reduced production
  • Avoid significant shading from trees, adjacent buildings, or structures
  • Our assessment includes shading analysis and production modeling

Structural Considerations:

  • Roof must support additional weight (~15-20 kg per square meter)
  • Heritage buildings may require specialized mounting solutions
  • Our team assesses structural capacity during site visit

Step 5: Match Budget to Goals

Conservative Approach:

  • Start with smaller system (3kW) that fits budget
  • Design electrical infrastructure for future expansion
  • Add panels later as budget allows or usage grows

Optimal Approach:

  • Size system to cover 70-90% of current usage
  • Maximize immediate savings and net metering benefits
  • Consider battery addition later if backup becomes priority

Premium Approach:

  • Size system to cover 100%+ of usage with export potential
  • Include battery storage for complete energy independence
  • Highest upfront cost but maximum long-term value

Keeping Your Solar System Performing: Maintenance and Warranty Guide

Solar systems require minimal maintenance but benefit from regular care. Here is what to expect for long-term performance and protection.

Routine Maintenance Tasks (Homeowner):

Monthly:

  • Visual inspection of panels for obvious debris, bird droppings, or damage
  • Check monitoring app for expected production vs. actual output
  • Note any unusual alerts or error messages from inverter

Quarterly:

  • Gentle cleaning of panels if dust or pollen accumulation is visible (use soft brush, water, no harsh chemicals)
  • Trim any new vegetation that may cause shading
  • Verify that mounting hardware remains secure and weatherproof

Annually:

  • Professional inspection recommended (included in maintenance contracts)
  • Inverter ventilation check and cleaning if accessible
  • Review annual production report against expectations

Professional Maintenance Services (Six7 Electrical):

Basic Maintenance Visit (Annual):

  • Comprehensive panel cleaning with appropriate equipment
  • Electrical connection inspection and tightening if needed
  • Inverter diagnostic check and firmware updates if available
  • Monitoring system verification and performance report

Advanced Maintenance (Every 3-5 Years):

  • Detailed electrical testing: insulation resistance, grounding verification
  • Thermal imaging to identify potential hotspots or failing components

Inverters:

  • Six7 Support: We can access monitoring data remotely to diagnose issues proactively

When to Consider System Upgrades:

  • Expanding panel array for increased usage (EV, home expansion)
  • Upgrading inverter for smart features or improved efficiency
  • Integrating with new home automation or energy management systems

Solar Power Myths vs. Facts: What Colombo Homeowners Should Know

Misinformation about solar power can lead to poor decisions. Here are common myths and the facts based on Sri Lankan experience.

Myth 1: Solar Panels Do Not Work Well in Monsoon Season

Fact: Solar panels produce electricity whenever there is daylight, including cloudy or rainy conditions. While production is reduced during heavy monsoon rains (typically 10-25% of peak), Colombo still receives sufficient annual sunlight for strong solar economics. Modern panels are rated for tropical weather and include weatherproof mounting.

Myth 2: Solar Systems Require Constant Maintenance and Cleaning

Fact: Solar systems are remarkably low-maintenance. Rain naturally cleans panels in most cases. Occasional cleaning (1-2 times per year) may optimize production in dusty areas, but systems perform well with minimal intervention. Professional maintenance visits are optional but recommended for peak performance.

Myth 3: Solar Power Will Not Work During Load-Shedding or Power Outages

Fact: Standard grid-tied systems automatically shut off during grid outages for safety (anti-islanding). However, hybrid systems with battery backup continue providing power to critical loads during outages. If backup power is important to you, specify a hybrid system during consultation.

Myth 4: Solar Panels Will Damage My Roof or Void My Roof Warranty

Fact: Professional solar installation includes weatherproof mounting that protects your roof. In many cases, panels shield roof materials from UV exposure and weather, potentially extending roof life. Reputable installers like Six7 Electrical coordinate with roofing warranties and provide documentation for your records.

Myth 5: Solar Systems Are Too Expensive for Average Homeowners

Fact: While upfront costs exist, solar systems typically pay for themselves in 4-6 years through electricity savings. After payback, decades of reduced or eliminated electricity bills represent significant long-term value. Financing options and phased installation can make solar accessible to various budgets.

Myth 6: Net Metering Credits Are Complicated or Unreliable

Fact: CEB net metering is a well-established program with clear procedures. Credits appear on your monthly bill automatically once the system is active. Six7 Electrical handles the entire application process and provides ongoing support for any billing questions.

Myth 7: Solar Technology Will Become Obsolete Soon, So I Should Wait

Fact: Solar technology evolves gradually, not disruptively. Current panels and inverters represent mature, reliable technology with 25-year performance warranties. Waiting for "next generation" technology means missing years of savings. Install when it makes financial sense for your situation.

Myth 8: Solar Installation Is Disruptive and Takes Weeks

Fact: Most residential solar installations in Colombo are completed in 2-5 days with minimal disruption. Professional installers coordinate with your schedule, protect your property, and clean up thoroughly. Power interruptions are brief and scheduled in advance.

Frequently Asked Questions About Solar Power in Sri Lanka

How much electricity will a solar system generate in Colombo?
A well-installed 3kW system in Colombo typically produces 4,000-4,500 kWh annually, depending on roof orientation, shading, and panel efficiency. This averages 11-12 kWh per day, enough to cover significant portions of typical household usage. We provide personalized production estimates during consultation.
What happens to my solar system during a power outage?
Standard grid-tied systems automatically shut off during grid outages for safety. If backup power is important, choose a hybrid system with battery storage, which continues powering critical loads during outages. We discuss your backup needs during system design.
Can I install solar panels myself to save money?
No. Solar installation requires licensed electrical expertise, grid compliance knowledge, and safety certifications. DIY installation risks voiding equipment warranties, creating safety hazards, and violating CEB regulations. Always use a licensed contractor like Six7 Electrical for safe, compliant installation.
How long does the CEB net metering approval process take?
Typical approval timelines range from 2-6 weeks after application submission, depending on CEB workload and application completeness. We prepare thorough applications to minimize delays and keep you informed throughout the process. System installation can proceed while net metering processes.
Will solar panels increase my property value?
Yes. Studies show solar installations typically increase property values by the present value of expected energy savings. In Sri Lanka's rising electricity cost environment, solar-equipped properties are increasingly attractive to buyers. We provide documentation to support property valuation.
What if I move or sell my property after installing solar?
Solar systems typically transfer with property ownership. Net metering accounts can be transferred to new owners with CEB approval. We provide complete documentation to facilitate smooth transfers. Solar installations often make properties more marketable and can be highlighted in sales listings.
Do solar panels work during monsoon season?
Yes. Solar panels produce electricity whenever there is daylight, including cloudy or rainy conditions. Production is reduced during heavy rain (typically 10-25% of peak), but Colombo's annual sunlight is sufficient for strong solar economics. Panels are rated for tropical weather and include weatherproof mounting.
How do I know if my roof is suitable for solar panels?
During consultation, we assess roof orientation, shading, structural capacity, and accessibility. South-facing roofs with minimal shading are ideal, but east or west-facing roofs can still be viable. We provide honest assessment and recommend solutions or alternatives if your roof has constraints.
