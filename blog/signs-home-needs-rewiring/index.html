5 Signs Your Colombo Home Needs Professional Rewiring

Older homes often have outdated electrical systems. Learn the warning signs that indicate your property needs rewiring to prevent fire hazards, electrocution risks, and costly damage.

Six7 Electrical Team

Updated: February 25, 2026 · 9 min read

Electrician inspecting old wiring in Colombo heritage home

Colombo is home to many beautiful heritage properties, colonial bungalows, and older residential buildings. While these homes have character and charm, their electrical systems often lag behind modern safety standards and power demands.

Electrical wiring has a typical lifespan of 20-40 years depending on materials, installation quality, and environmental conditions. In Colombo's tropical climate – with high humidity, monsoon exposure, and temperature fluctuations – wiring can deteriorate faster than in drier climates.

At Six7 Electrical, we have assessed and rewired dozens of older homes across Colombo 01-15 and surrounding suburbs. We understand the unique challenges of heritage properties: preserving architectural character while upgrading electrical safety, working with limited access points, and complying with modern Sri Lankan electrical standards.

This guide covers the 5 most common warning signs that your Colombo home may need professional rewiring:

  • Frequent breaker tripping or blown fuses
  • Flickering or dimming lights without obvious cause
  • Burning smells, warm outlets, or visible discoloration
  • Outlets that do not work, spark, or feel loose
  • Home built before 1980 or known knob-and-tube wiring

If you recognize any of these signs, it is time to schedule a professional electrical safety inspection. Ignoring warning signs can lead to fire hazards, equipment damage, or electrocution risks.

1

Your Circuit Breaker Trips Repeatedly

Occasional breaker tripping during extreme weather or appliance use can be normal. Frequent or unexplained tripping signals an underlying electrical problem.

What It Means:

  • Circuit overload: Too many devices drawing power on one circuit
  • Short circuit: Live and neutral wires touching, creating dangerous current surge
  • Ground fault: Current leaking to ground, often due to damaged insulation or moisture
  • Failing breaker: The breaker itself may be worn out and need replacement

Why It Matters in Older Colombo Homes:

  • Original wiring was designed for lower electrical loads (fewer appliances, no air conditioning)
  • Modern households use far more power: multiple AC units, computers, kitchen appliances, EV chargers
  • Aging insulation can crack or degrade, causing intermittent shorts
  • Monsoon humidity can accelerate deterioration of old wiring

What to Do:

  • Do not repeatedly reset a tripping breaker without investigation
  • Note which circuits trip and what appliances were in use
  • Reduce load on the affected circuit temporarily
  • Schedule a professional electrical assessment with Six7 Electrical

Red Flags That Require Immediate Attention:

  • Breaker trips immediately after resetting
  • Tripping accompanied by burning smell, sparks, or unusual sounds
  • Multiple circuits tripping simultaneously
  • Breaker panel feels hot to the touch

Lights Flicker or Dim Without Obvious Cause

Lights may flicker briefly when a high-power appliance starts. Persistent or widespread flickering indicates a wiring problem.

Common Causes in Older Homes:

  • Loose connections: Wires at outlets, switches, or the distribution board may have worked loose over time
  • Overloaded circuits: Too many devices on one circuit cause voltage drops that affect lighting
  • Deteriorating wiring: Aging insulation or corroded conductors create inconsistent power flow
  • Faulty switches or outlets: Worn internal components cause intermittent contact

Why It Is Dangerous:

  • Loose connections generate heat, which can ignite surrounding materials
  • Voltage fluctuations can damage sensitive electronics and appliances
  • Intermittent power can indicate impending complete circuit failure

Colombo-Specific Factors:

  • Monsoon humidity accelerates corrosion of metal contacts and connections
  • Temperature fluctuations cause expansion/contraction that can loosen older wiring
  • Heritage homes may have original cloth-insulated wiring that becomes brittle with age

What to Check Before Calling:

  • Does flickering happen only when specific appliances turn on? (May indicate overload)
  • Is it isolated to one room or circuit? (May indicate localized issue)
  • Does it happen during heavy rain or high humidity? (May indicate moisture ingress)

When to Call a Professional:

  • Flickering is widespread or affects multiple circuits
  • Lights dim significantly (not just brief flicker)
  • Problem persists after reducing load on the circuit
  • You notice any burning smell, warmth, or discoloration near outlets or switches

Burning Smell, Warm Outlets, or Discoloration

These are serious warning signs that require immediate attention. Do not ignore them.

Burning or Fishy Smell:

  • Cause: Overheating wires, melting insulation, or arcing electricity
  • Danger: Can precede electrical fire; smoke may contain toxic fumes
  • Action: Turn off power at main breaker if safe, evacuate area, call Six7 Electrical immediately

Warm or Hot Outlets/Switches:

  • Cause: Loose connections, overloaded circuits, or failing components generating heat
  • Danger: Heat can ignite surrounding materials; indicates active fault
  • Action: Stop using the outlet, do not touch if hot, schedule urgent professional assessment

Discoloration or Scorch Marks:

  • Cause: Arcing, overheating, or past minor electrical fires
  • Danger: Indicates damage that may worsen; compromised insulation increases shock risk
  • Action: Document with photos, avoid using affected outlets, contact Six7 Electrical for inspection

Why Older Homes Are at Higher Risk:

  • Original wiring may use materials no longer considered safe (e.g., rubber insulation, aluminum conductors)
  • Outlets and switches from decades past lack modern safety features like arc-fault protection
  • Previous DIY repairs or modifications may have introduced unsafe practices

Immediate Safety Steps:

  1. Turn off power to affected circuit at distribution board if you can do so safely
  2. Unplug devices from affected outlets
  3. Do not attempt to repair yourself unless you are a licensed electrician
  4. Call Six7 Electrical at +94 75 824 4216 for emergency assessment

Outlets That Do Not Work or Spark

Outlets should work reliably. Intermittent operation, sparks, or complete failure signals wiring problems.

Types of Outlet Problems:

  • Dead outlets: No power despite breaker being on – indicates broken wire, loose connection, or failed outlet
  • Intermittent outlets: Work sometimes, not others – suggests loose connection or deteriorating wiring
  • Sparking outlets: Visible sparks when plugging/unplugging – indicates arcing, serious fire risk
  • Loose outlets: Plug does not fit snugly – worn internal contacts can overheat

Why This Happens in Older Colombo Homes:

  • Original outlets may be 30-50 years old with worn internal mechanisms
  • Wiring connections behind outlets can loosen over decades of use
  • Humidity and temperature changes accelerate corrosion of metal contacts
  • Previous renovations may have damaged wiring when cutting into walls

Safety Risks:

  • Arcing at outlets can ignite nearby materials (curtains, furniture, wall coverings)
  • Loose connections generate heat that can melt insulation and cause fires
  • Faulty grounding increases electrocution risk, especially in wet areas like kitchens and bathrooms

What to Do:

  • Stop using sparking or intermittently working outlets immediately
  • Do not force plugs into loose outlets; this worsens internal damage
  • Test GFCI outlets (if present) using the test/reset buttons
  • Schedule a professional outlet inspection and replacement with Six7 Electrical

Your Home Is Older or Has Outdated Wiring Types

Age alone does not guarantee wiring problems, but older homes have higher risk. Certain wiring types are now considered unsafe.

Knob-and-Tube Wiring:

  • What it is: Early 20th century system using porcelain knobs and tubes to support single-insulated wires
  • Why it is problematic: No ground wire, insulation becomes brittle, not designed for modern loads, difficult to insure
  • How to identify: Visible in attics or basements as white ceramic knobs with wires running through tubes

Other Outdated Systems in Colombo Homes:

  • Rubber-insulated wiring: Becomes brittle and cracks with age, exposing live conductors
  • Aluminum wiring (1960s-1970s): Prone to oxidation and loose connections at terminals
  • Two-prong ungrounded outlets: Lack safety ground, incompatible with modern three-prong plugs

Why Age Matters Even Without Obvious Problems:

  • Wiring insulation degrades over time even without visible damage
  • Electrical codes have evolved; older systems may not meet current safety standards
  • Modern power demands (AC, computers, EV chargers) exceed original design capacity
  • Insurance companies may require rewiring for coverage on very old systems

How to Check Your Home's Wiring Age:

  • Review property documentation or renovation records
  • Look at outlets and switches: two-prong outlets often indicate pre-1970s wiring
  • Check distribution board: old fuse boxes (vs. modern MCB panels) suggest older system

Why Professional Assessment Matters:

Frequently Asked Questions About Home Rewiring in Colombo

How do I know if my home needs full rewiring or just repairs?
A professional electrical safety inspection is the only reliable way to determine scope. We assess wiring condition, load capacity, safety compliance, and your usage needs to recommend targeted repairs or full rewiring. Many homes benefit from a hybrid approach: critical circuits rewired first, with phased expansion.
Will rewiring damage my heritage home's character or finishes?
We specialize in minimal-invasive techniques for heritage properties: fishing wires through existing cavities, using original access points, and carefully restoring any minor access areas. We collaborate with conservation specialists when required to preserve architectural character while upgrading electrical safety.
How long does a full home rewiring take?
Typical timelines: small homes 2-3 days, medium homes 4-5 days, large or heritage homes 5-7 days. We coordinate scheduling with your routine, provide daily progress updates, and minimize power interruptions to essential areas.
Do I need to move out during rewiring?
Most homeowners can remain in the property during rewiring with minor adjustments. We phase work to maintain access to essential areas (one bathroom, kitchen, bedroom) and schedule power interruptions in advance. For extensive projects, we can discuss temporary accommodation options.
What warranties do you provide on rewiring work?
All rewiring includes our 90-day workmanship guarantee covering installation quality. New wiring materials carry manufacturer warranties (typically 10-25 years for cables, 2-5 years for outlets/switches). We provide documentation for insurance and future reference.
Will rewiring increase my home insurance premium or coverage?
Updated electrical systems often qualify for insurance premium discounts and ensure coverage eligibility. Many insurers require electrical upgrades for homes over a certain age. We provide compliance documentation to support insurance applications or claims.
