Burning Smell or Sparks from Outlet

Smoke, burning odor, or visible sparks from a socket or switch indicates dangerous overheating or short circuit. Turn off mains and call us immediately.

Symptoms We Fix:

  • Visible sparks or arcing from outlets or switches
  • Burning plastic or fishy smell from electrical points
  • Discolored or melted switch plates
  • Warm or hot outlets to the touch

Our Process:

  • 1. Immediate safety instructions over phone (turn off mains, unplug devices)
  • 2. Rapid dispatch of technician with fire suppression knowledge
  • 3. Isolate faulty circuit and diagnose root cause
  • 4. Replace damaged outlets, switches, or wiring
  • 5. Test for safety and provide prevention advice

When to Call Us Immediately:

  • You see sparks or smoke from any electrical point
  • You smell burning coming from walls or outlets
  • An outlet feels hot or shows scorch marks

Safety Note: If you see flames, evacuate and call the fire department first, then call us.

Circuit Breaker Keeps Tripping

Repeated tripping of your trip switch or MCB indicates an overload, short circuit, or earth fault. We identify the root cause and fix it safely.

Symptoms We Fix:

  • Breaker trips immediately after resetting
  • Breaker trips when specific appliances are used
  • Intermittent tripping with no obvious pattern
  • Multiple breakers tripping simultaneously

Our Process:

  • 1. Phone diagnosis to identify likely cause (appliance vs wiring)
  • 2. Technician arrives with load testing equipment
  • 3. Systematic circuit isolation to locate fault
  • 4. Repair short circuit, replace faulty MCB, or rebalance loads
  • 5. Test under load to ensure stable operation

Common Causes We Address:

  • Overloaded circuits from high-draw appliances
  • Short circuit in wiring or connected devices
  • Earth leakage due to moisture or damaged insulation
  • Faulty or aged MCB requiring replacement

When to Call Immediately:

  • Breaker trips repeatedly within minutes of resetting
  • Tripping is accompanied by sparks, smells, or sounds
  • You cannot identify which appliance is causing the issue

Storm or Monsoon Flood Electrical Damage

Water and electricity do not mix. After heavy rain or flooding, we safely inspect, dry, and restore your electrical system to prevent shocks or fire.

Symptoms We Fix:

  • Power loss after heavy rain or flooding
  • Water ingress into outdoor outlets or meter boxes
  • Damp or wet electrical panels
  • Lightning strike damage to electrical systems

Our Process:

  • 1. Safety-first assessment: ensure area is safe to enter
  • 2. Isolate affected circuits and dry components professionally
  • 3. Test insulation resistance and replace compromised wiring
  • 4. Restore power with safety verification
  • 5. Recommend preventive measures for future monsoons

When to Call Us Immediately:

  • Flooding has reached electrical outlets or panels
  • You suspect water has entered your meter box
  • Lightning strike caused immediate power loss
  • Outdoor lighting or sockets are submerged or wet

Safety Note: Do not touch electrical components with wet hands or while standing in water.

Partial Power Loss – Some Rooms Working

Lights working in one room but not another points to a circuit-specific fault. We trace the issue in your distribution board or wiring.

Symptoms We Fix:

  • Some outlets or lights work while others do not
  • Power loss limited to one floor or section of home
  • Intermittent power in specific rooms
  • GFCI outlets tripping and not resetting

Our Process:

  • 1. Map affected circuits via phone questioning
  • 2. Technician tests each circuit at the distribution board
  • 3. Trace wiring to locate break, short, or loose connection
  • 4. Repair or replace faulty wiring or components
  • 5. Verify all circuits are stable and safe

Common Causes We Address:

  • Loose neutral or live connection in junction box
  • Damaged wiring behind walls or under floors
  • Faulty GFCI or RCD outlet
  • Overloaded circuit causing partial failure

When to Call Immediately:

  • Power loss is expanding to more rooms
  • You notice flickering or dimming in working circuits
  • The issue started after recent renovation or drilling

Recent construction causing flickering lights, tripping breakers, or dead outlets? We inspect and correct unsafe or non-compliant electrical installations.

Symptoms We Fix:

  • Electrical issues appearing after renovation work
  • New outlets or switches not functioning correctly
  • Breakers tripping after adding new appliances or circuits

  • Non-compliant junction boxes or connections

When to Call Immediately:

Frequently Asked Questions – Emergency Electrical Help

How fast can you respond to an electrical emergency in Colombo?

Do you charge a call-out fee for emergency electrical repairs?
No. Six7 Electrical never charges a call-out fee for emergency electrical repairs – ever. You only pay for the work performed, with transparent pricing approved before we begin. This policy applies to all emergencies: power outages, short circuits, breaker issues, storm damage, and more. For scheduled, non-emergency work, a standard diagnostic fee may apply (waived if you proceed with the repair).
What should I do while waiting for the emergency electrician?
First, ensure safety: turn off the main circuit breaker if you smell burning or see sparks. Unplug sensitive electronics to protect them from surges. Stay away from water if flooding is involved. Keep pets and children clear of the affected area. Have your address, contact details, and a description of the problem ready for our technician. Do not attempt repairs yourself unless you are a qualified electrician.
Are your emergency electricians licensed and insured?
Yes. All Six7 technicians undergo regular safety training and carry comprehensive public liability insurance for your protection. We maintain the highest industry standards for emergency electrical work in Sri Lanka. You can request to see our technician identification and insurance documentation upon arrival.
What if you do not have the part needed for my emergency repair?
Our emergency vans stock common parts for urgent fixes: MCBs, outlets, switches, wiring, and connectors. If a specialized part is required, we will source it from our certified suppliers and return to complete the job – often the same day. You will never be charged for a second call-out for the same issue. We will provide a clear timeline and cost estimate before ordering any parts.
Do you offer warranties on emergency repair work?
Yes. All emergency repairs come with a 90-day workmanship guarantee. If the same issue reoccurs within that period due to our work, we will return and fix it at no additional cost. Parts carry manufacturer warranties (typically 1-2 years), which we will explain and document at the time of repair.
