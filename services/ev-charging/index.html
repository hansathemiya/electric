Frequently Asked Questions – EV Charging Installation

How long does it take to charge my EV at home?
Charging time depends on your vehicle's battery size and the charger's power output. With a Level 2 home charger (7.4 kW), most EVs gain 25-50 km of range per hour of charging. A typical overnight charge (8 hours) can fully recharge most vehicles for daily commuting. We help you select the right charger for your driving patterns.
Do I need to upgrade my electrical panel for EV charging?
Not always. We assess your existing panel capacity during consultation. If sufficient capacity exists, we install a dedicated circuit for the charger. If capacity is limited, we recommend load management systems that enable charging without panel upgrades. Major upgrades are only recommended when necessary for safety and reliability.
Can I charge my EV using solar power?
Yes. We can integrate EV charging with existing or new solar power systems. Smart chargers can be configured to prioritize solar energy when available, reducing grid dependence and charging costs. We assess your solar system capacity and recommend appropriate integration solutions.
Is it safe to install an EV charger outdoors in Colombo's climate?
Yes. We install weatherproof chargers with appropriate IP ratings for outdoor use in tropical climates. All outdoor installations include weatherproof enclosures, proper cable protection, and secure mounting to withstand monsoon conditions. We select equipment rated for Sri Lankan weather patterns.
Can I install a charger now and add more later?
Absolutely. We design installations with future expansion in mind. For home installations, we can pre-wire for additional chargers. For commercial properties, we recommend scalable load management systems that support adding chargers without major infrastructure changes.
Do you handle CEB approval or net metering for EV charging?
Yes. For installations requiring formal approval or net metering coordination, we provide documentation support and can coordinate with CEB on your behalf. Most residential Level 2 chargers do not require special approval, but we guide you through any necessary steps.
